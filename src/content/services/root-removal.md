The driveway didn't crack on its own. That slow ridge lifting the concrete, the hairline in the slab's corner, the shower that drains a little worse each month — around Pasadena, there's often a root under all of it. Our clay soil keeps moisture near the surface, so roots stay shallow, run long, and head straight for anything wet or easy.

Root removal deals with the specific root causing the specific problem — without condemning the tree, and without excavating half the yard to find it.

## How do you remove a root without killing the tree?

By treating it as arborist work, not demolition. First we trace the root and confirm it belongs to the tree everyone suspects — homeowners guess wrong more than you'd think. Then it's cut at the greatest survivable distance from the trunk, extracted back past the problem area, and finished cleanly so decay doesn't run up the cut. The [ISA's homeowner guidance](https://www.treesaregood.org/treeowner) frames root loss as a budget the tree can only spend once; we spend it carefully. When the math doesn't work — a huge root feeding half the canopy, headed under the foundation — we'll say plainly that the honest fix is [tree removal](/services/tree-removal/), not a cut that destabilizes a 60-footer next to your house.

## Will the root just grow back?

The cut root won't reconnect, but the tree will send new growth toward the same water if nothing stops it. That's what root barriers are for: a physical wall installed in the trench that redirects future growth down and away from the slab, the pool, or the line you just protected. Barrier plus removal is the durable combination; removal alone is a several-year reprieve.

Root problems rarely travel alone, so it pairs naturally with the rest of the ground-level work: [stump grinding](/services/stump-grinding/) when the tree is already gone, full [stump removal](/services/stump-grinding/stump-removal/) when an entire root system has to come out for construction, and [canopy trimming](/services/tree-trimming/canopy-trimming/) when the same tree crowding your slab is also crowding your roof.
