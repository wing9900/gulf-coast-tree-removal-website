Houston is a forest with a city in it, and the southeast side we serve is some of the oldest canopy in town. From our shop in Pasadena we run crews up SH 225 and I-45 into the East End, Glenbrook Valley, and the neighborhoods around Hobby Airport, where live oaks planted generations ago now stand over single-story roofs, driveways, and every wire on the block.

## Can you remove a big tree from a tight Houston lot?

Yes, that is the job we are built for. Houston's older southeast neighborhoods mean narrow side yards, back fences with no gate access, and trees far too big to fell in one piece. A [large tree removal](/services/tree-removal/large-tree-removal/) here is climbed, rigged, and lowered a section at a time, planned by an ISA Certified Arborist before anyone starts a saw. If the tree is already failing, dead, or storm-cracked, it needs the [hazardous tree removal](/services/tree-removal/hazardous-tree-removal/) treatment and a faster phone call.

## How should Houston live oaks be trimmed?

To the ANSI A300 standard, which is the written standard cities and utilities hold their own contractors to. Our [canopy trimming](/services/tree-trimming/canopy-trimming/) lifts the crown off your roof, clears the streetlights and sightlines, and never tops a tree. The Tree Care Industry Association, which publishes those standards, explains what professional tree care should look like at [tcia.org](https://www.tcia.org/).

## What about the mess a Houston storm leaves behind?

We haul it. After hurricanes and derecho winds, city pickup schedules stretch out for weeks, and a limb pile at the curb becomes a fixture. Our [debris removal](/services/land-clearing/debris-removal/) crews chip and haul storm wood off your property in one visit, and the [wood chipping](/services/land-clearing/wood-chipping/) can leave you mulch if you want it.
