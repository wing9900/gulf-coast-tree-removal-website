La Porte sits between our Pasadena shop and Galveston Bay, about 15 minutes down Spencer Highway, and it gets weather the rest of the area only reads about. When a front stalls over the bay or a hurricane pushes water past Sylvan Beach, La Porte trees take the first and hardest wind. An ISA Certified Arborist prices every La Porte job in person, free, with nothing owed until the work is done.

## What does bay wind do to trees in La Porte?

It finds the weak wood first, every time. Homes near Sylvan Beach and along the Bayshore stand in unbroken wind off Galveston Bay, and the water oaks and pines in Fairmont Park are now tall enough that a dead limb over the roof is a genuine roof claim waiting on a gust. [Storm preparation trimming](/services/tree-trimming/storm-preparation-trimming/) before June removes the dead, cracked, and overextended limbs that storms tear loose, and it is a fraction of the cost of the cleanup call.

Anyone who was in La Porte for Hurricane Ike remembers what the bay can do. A tree that is already leaning, hollow, or browning does not get better odds next season, which is exactly what [hazardous tree removal](/services/tree-removal/hazardous-tree-removal/) is for.

## Do La Porte's old oaks deserve an arborist instead of a tree guy?

Yes, because some of the oldest live oaks on the Gulf Coast grow right here. The trees shading the [San Jacinto Battleground](https://www.sanjacinto-museum.org/) were standing before Texas was Texas, and the mature oaks around the Main Street district are part of what makes old La Porte feel like old La Porte. Work on trees like these follows the ANSI A300 standard on every cut: no topping, no spikes in a living tree, never more than a quarter of the canopy in a season.

## Can you clear acreage and overgrown lots in Lomax?
