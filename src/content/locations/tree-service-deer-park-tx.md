## What happens to a dying tree in Deer Park if you wait?

It picks its own moment to fail, usually in the first hard blow of the season. Deer Park's neighborhoods went in decades ago, and the trees planted with them are now big enough that a failure means a roof, a fence, or a truck. A leaning trunk, a dead crown, or mushrooms at the base are reasons to get a [hazardous tree removal](/services/tree-removal/hazardous-tree-removal/) assessment now, while it is still a scheduled job instead of an [emergency call](/services/tree-removal/emergency-tree-removal/).

## When should Deer Park trees be trimmed for storm season?

Before June, when hurricane season starts in earnest. [Storm preparation trimming](/services/tree-trimming/storm-preparation-trimming/) takes out the dead wood, the cracked limbs, and the overextended branches over the roof, which are the pieces that fail first in wind. The [National Weather Service Houston/Galveston office](https://www.weather.gov/hgx/) is the place to watch the forecasts; our job is making sure your trees are ready before those forecasts matter.

## Is regular pruning worth it for a healthy Deer Park tree?

Yes, because pruning done right is what keeps the tree healthy and the big bills away. Our [tree pruning](/services/tree-trimming/tree-pruning/) follows the ANSI A300 standard on every cut: branch collar cuts, no topping, no spikes in a living tree, never more than a quarter of the canopy in a season.
