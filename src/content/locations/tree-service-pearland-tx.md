Pearland has grown faster than almost any city on the south side of Houston, and its trees tell the story twice. The Old Townsite streets have mature oaks and pecans that need real arborist care, while the newer subdivisions off Shadow Creek Parkway have builder-planted trees now big enough to cause their first problems. We cover all of it from our Pasadena shop, about half an hour up Beltway 8, with an ISA Certified Arborist on every job.

## What tree problems come with Pearland's clay soil?

Roots, mostly. Pearland yards sit on expansive clay that swells and shrinks with the rain, and thirsty roots follow the moisture straight under driveways, patios, and foundation slabs. If a tree was planted too close to concrete, a [root barrier installation](/services/tree-health/root-barrier-installation/) redirects the roots down and away before the slab pays for it. Where the damage argument is already lost, [root removal](/services/stump-grinding/root-removal/) takes out the surface roots that are doing the lifting.

## Do you clear lots for new construction in Pearland?

Yes, and Pearland still has plenty of wooded parcels going under new homes and shops. Our [lot clearing](/services/land-clearing/lot-clearing/) crews take out the trees, grind or extract the stumps, and haul the debris so the pad is genuinely ready. Texas A&M Forest Service, the state's forestry agency, has good guidance on which trees are worth designing a build around at [tfsweb.tamu.edu](https://tfsweb.tamu.edu/).
