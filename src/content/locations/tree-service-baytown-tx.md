## Can you handle the big water oaks and pines in Baytown's older neighborhoods?

Yes, and they are the trees we see most in Baytown. The established streets around Wooster and Goose Creek grew their shade for fifty years or more, and a water oak that size does not come down in one piece. It comes down in sections, rigged and lowered, which is exactly what our [large tree removal](/services/tree-removal/large-tree-removal/) crews do week in and week out.

If the tree is dead, cracked, or leaning toward the house, treat it as a [hazardous tree removal](/services/tree-removal/hazardous-tree-removal/) and get it looked at before the next storm makes the decision for you.

## What do Gulf storms do to Baytown trees?

Baytown takes hurricane wind off the bay with less to slow it down than almost anywhere else we serve. The [National Weather Service in Houston](https://www.weather.gov/hgx/) tracks these storms closely, and after each one, the calls from Baytown come in waves: snapped pines, split oaks, limbs through fences.

The cheaper option is the work done before the storm. [Storm preparation trimming](/services/tree-trimming/storm-preparation-trimming/) removes the dead wood and overextended limbs that fail first, and it costs a fraction of an emergency call.
