Clear Lake grew up with the space program, and so did its trees. The neighborhoods built around NASA's [Johnson Space Center](https://www.nasa.gov/johnson/) in the 1960s and 70s were planted with loblolly pines and oaks that are now sixty feet tall and standing over one-story roofs from Clear Lake City to Bay Area Blvd. We run crews down from Pasadena in about 15 minutes, with an ISA Certified Arborist reading every tree first.

## What is killing the tall pines in Clear Lake?

Most often pine bark beetles moving into trees already stressed by drought or root damage. By the time a Clear Lake pine browns from the top, the beetles have usually finished it, and a dead loblolly gets brittle fast. Catching the neighbors' outbreak early is the whole game: [insect and borer treatment](/services/tree-health/insect-borer-treatment/) can protect the pines still worth saving, and [tree disease treatment](/services/tree-health/tree-disease-treatment/) sorts out what is actually wrong before anything is cut.

## How does a 60-foot pine come down between Clear Lake houses?

In sections, rigged from the top, with the arborist calling the order of cuts. Clear Lake's patio homes and cul-de-sacs leave no room to fell anything whole, so [large tree removal](/services/tree-removal/large-tree-removal/) here is climbing work: piece by piece, lowered on ropes, with the lawn intact when the trailer leaves. Stump grinding is included, not extra.

## Can a healthy but oversized tree be made storm-safer without removing it?

Often, yes. [Crown reduction](/services/tree-trimming/crown-reduction/) shortens the canopy to sound lateral limbs so hurricane wind has less to grab, which matters this close to Galveston Bay. It is proper ANSI A300 work, not topping, and the tree keeps its health and its shape.
